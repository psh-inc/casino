# Compliance Requirements - Anjouan Gaming License

These requirements are derived from the sources in regulatory-sources.md. The system SHALL comply with these requirements.

## Licensing and governance

- The licensee SHALL comply with the Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005 (Online Gaming Act) (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The operator SHALL not provide or assist in providing computer gaming services without a license issued by the Board (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 3).
- The operator SHALL apply to the Board for a license before taking steps to provide a computer wagering service (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 6).
- The licensee SHALL submit reports to the Gaming Board at intervals and in the format determined by the Board (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL meet financial ratio requirements as notified by the Gaming Board (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL notify the Gaming Board within 7 days of any beneficial ownership change of 3 percent or more (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL notify the Gaming Board of any gaming license termination by any jurisdiction (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL notify the Gaming Board within 48 hours of any serious incident impacting operations or Anjouan's reputation (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL notify the Gaming Board of any material change in information supplied during licensing (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL attend meetings of the Gaming Board with reasonable notice (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL satisfy the Gaming Board that its systems supplier is a fit and proper business associate (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The Gaming Board/OFA MAY suspend the license if renewal fees are not received by the due date (General Gaming License Conditions).

## Responsible gambling and advertising

- The licensee SHALL maintain procedures to identify and support players at risk of compulsive gambling (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL provide counseling/support information to at-risk players (General Gaming License Conditions).
- Advertising SHALL NOT promote under-aged gaming or bring Anjouan into disrepute (General Gaming License Conditions).

## International operations

- The licensee SHALL keep abreast of international legal developments and avoid operating where interactive gaming is criminalized (General Gaming License Conditions).

## AML and financial controls

- Cash SHALL NOT be used in any transaction involving a player's account (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL establish a bank account to process computer user transactions (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8).
- Each computer user SHALL maintain deposited funds sufficient to cover wagers placed (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8).
- The licensee SHALL identify funds on deposit for each computer user (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8).
- Computer user deposits SHALL NOT be accepted in cash; deposits SHALL be by cheque, cashier's cheque, money order, wire transfer, or other electronic means (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8(4)).
- Computer user withdrawals SHALL NOT be paid in cash; withdrawals SHALL be by cheque, cashier's cheque, money order, wire transfer, or other electronic means (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8(5)).
- Total wagers placed by a computer user at any time SHALL NOT exceed the total funds deposited by that user (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8(7)).
- The licensee SHALL provide daily account reports of user transactions and balances when required by the Board (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8(8)).
- The licensee SHALL cap per-transaction service fees to 5% or USD 20 (whichever is greater) and deduct fees only when a wager is confirmed (Computer Gaming Licensing Act 007 of 2005, Section 8(9)).
- The licensee SHALL maintain AML procedures covering identification of players, record keeping, suspicious transaction reporting, payment controls, and staff training (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL appoint a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) who reports suspicious transactions to the Gaming Board (General Gaming License Conditions).
- The licensee SHALL appoint a compliance officer who reports regularly to the Gaming Board (General Gaming License Conditions).

## Record keeping and reporting (Money Laundering Act)

- Financial institutions SHALL keep business transaction records, including identification of parties, transaction purpose, account details, and total value (Money Laundering Act 008 of 2005, Section 2 definitions).
- Financial institutions SHALL report suspicious transactions promptly to the Supervisory Authority (Money Laundering Act 008 of 2005, Section 13).
- Business transaction records related to suspicious transaction reports SHALL be retained for six years after termination of the business transaction (Money Laundering Act 008 of 2005, Obligations of Financial Institutions, section 12(j)).
